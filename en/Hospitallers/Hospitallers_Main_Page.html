History of the Order

The origins of the Order date back to the mid-11th century in Jerusalem, where some merchants from the maritime Republic of Amalfi obtained permission from the Caliph of Egypt to build a church, a convent, and a hospital to assist pilgrims of any faith or race.

List of Grand Masters

The Grand Master is the highest authority of the Order, elected for life. From the founder, Blessed Gerard, to the complex events of modern times, this figure has led the knights in battles, sieges, and international diplomacy.

Architecture and Fortifications

In addition to their hospitaller vocation, the Order developed formidable skills in military engineering. Castles like the Krak des Chevaliers or the imposing walls of the city of Valletta in Malta are direct testimonies of their defensive power.

Organization and "Langues"

With its growth throughout Europe, the Order adopted a unique supranational structure, dividing itself into "Langues" (Provence, Auvergne, France, Italy, Aragon, England, and Germany), each responsible for the defense of specific sectors during sieges.
